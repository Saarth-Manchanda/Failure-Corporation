How We Started

Failure Corporations began with a single, spectacular flop. Our founders, Saarth Manchanda and Aditya Bhardwaj, realized that true innovation often springs from the ashes of missteps. They met at a "Worst Ideas Ever" convention, where their shared passion for learning from catastrophic errors sparked the idea for this company.

From that initial, utterly disastrous product launch (we don't talk about the "Brush for a brush"), they dedicated themselves to understanding the anatomy of failure. They built a framework based on dissecting what went wrong, ensuring those lessons were not just learned, but enshrined as guiding principles for future failures... and eventually, failed. Our journey is a testament to the idea that sometimes, the best way forward is to fail multiple times .
